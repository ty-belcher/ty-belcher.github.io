2025 November

Phonoglyphics 2.1: "Hueish"

Nearly the same as 2.0, but instead of unique symbols, they've been replaced with colors. Originally I was going to go off of Baskin Robbins' 31 flavors to come up with the colors! Each color was fine-tuned to make sure they can stand out from each other and remain visible in front of black or white. Many color alphabets have been created before and have called attention to multiple caveats; mostly color blindness and differing viewing angles on LCD screens.

2025 August

Phonoglyphics 2.0

A reinvented, more "digital" version of the original Phonoglyphics I created previously. Rather than 65 symbols, there are 31 present here, with no symbols for numbers or punctuation and one other symbol for a blank space. Some phonemes that sounded too similar to others were conjoined into one symbol and other phonemes that can be derived from combinations of phonemes, like diphthongs, were cut entirely.

2024 April

Phonoglyphics

An conceptual alphabet where each symbol represents a different sound in a language, also known as a phoneme. The system uses mixtures of basic geometric shapes, while maintaining vertical symmetry. Each symbol’s design is also based on the type of phoneme being conveyed. Consonants use primarily straight and diagonal lines with no rounded parts, all vowels use circular bases, punctuations use rounded square bases, and numbers use a dual “pill shape.” It's based on the International Phonetic Alphabet, or the IPA, where each letter represents a different sound in a set amount of languages. Ever look up the definition of a word and see something below that word between two slashes? That’s a visualization of how the word is pronounced. The IPA, at least in most English charts, includes 44 different phonemes, so the first version of the alphabet contains 44 distinct symbols, plus 11 for punctuations and 10 for numbers, which adds up to 65 total glyphs.
